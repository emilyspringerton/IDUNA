VS12 Specification: Crafting & Material Transformation
VS12 represents a critical evolution in the IDUNA universe, transitioning players from passive consumers to authors of reality
. It is not a traditional "crafting" system found in typical games; it is a Material Transformation Layer that allows actors to reconfigure the persistent world state through deliberate agency
.
1. Core Principle: Reality Transformation
Nothing from Nowhere: Matter is never "spawned" magically. Every object must be a deterministic transformation of existing inputs
.
Controlled Manipulation: Crafting is the mechanism for reality manipulation, where the Event Store records the "memory of actions" and the True Store reflects the "current state"
.
2. Material Lineage Integration (VS13 Synergy)
The integrity of VS12 relies on the Material Lineage Model defined in VS13
.
Traceable Provenance: Inputs must be legitimate (harvested, purchased, or recycled)
.
Inherited Ancestry: Crafted outputs inherit the lineage of their components, providing deep hooks for economic simulation, fraud prevention, and historical inspection
.
3. Categories of Crafting (Initial Scope)
VS12 introduces three primary modes of transformation:
Transformative Crafting: Converting raw materials into refined versions or composite objects (e.g., Raw Material → Refined Material)
.
Constructive Crafting: Creating durable world artifacts such as devices, props, or environmental objects that may later populate areas like THE_CITY_OF_LIGHT
.
Cosmetic & Expressive Crafting: High-identity, low-risk items like insignias, symbolic objects, or "desk artifacts" that align with the system's archival visual language
.
4. Operational Model & Constraints
To prevent economic chaos and encourage specialization, crafting is governed by strict rules:
Deterministic Outputs: Every recipe must yield a predictable result to ensure replay safety and consistency
.
Location & Permission Constraints: Crafting actions may require specific world locations, permissions, or capabilities, naturally creating professions and social differentiation without forced class systems
.
Anti-Chaos Rules: The system must architecturally forbid infinite resource loops and client-authoritative recipes
.
5. Event Sourcing & Auditability
Every crafting action is an event-producing transaction. The canonical vocabulary includes:
CraftingJobStarted / CraftingJobCompleted
MaterialTransformed
ArtifactCreated / ArtifactConsumed
CraftingFailed
6. Economic & Social Impact
Economic Sinks: Crafting serves as a vital sink for resources, stabilizing virtual economies by driving trade and scarcity
.
Institutional Memory: All significant creations are reflected in VS10 Scoreboards, treated as socially authoritative records rather than gamified "achievements"
.
7. Visual & Aesthetic Direction: The "Institutional Workshop"
The UI for crafting must avoid arcade clichés (glittering wheels or forging minigames) and adhere to the "Back Office" aesthetic
.
Tactile Mechanics: The interface should feel like a procedural workshop, bench, or terminal
.
Calm & Procedural: Use muted tones, structural lines, and a sense of "mechanical rhythm" consistent with an institutional apparatus for material change
.
8. VS12 "Done" Definition
A successful implementation of VS12 is verified when:
A functional Material Lineage Model is in place
.
The system emits canonical events for all transformations
.
Deterministic transformation rules are enforced by the server
.
Permission and capability checks are integrated into the workflow
.
The UI matches the institutional/archival tone of the IDUNA ecosystem
.
