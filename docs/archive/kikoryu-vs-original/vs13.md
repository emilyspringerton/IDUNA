VS13 Specification: Source Material Lineage & Provenance
VS13 serves as the anti-chaos backbone for the IDUNA ecosystem, ensuring that every object in the world has a traceable, legitimate origin
. It is the stabilizing layer that prevents magical item generation, duplication exploits, and economic instability by providing causal integrity and institutional realism
.
1. Core Principle: The Ontological Rule
The fundamental law of VS13 is that no artifact exists without origin and no material exists without a source
. In this event-sourced universe, materials are not merely rows in a database; they are historical entities that carry their full material ancestry with them
.
2. Material Identity Model
Materials in VS13 behave as atomic value-bearing units with ancestry, rather than generic stackable blobs
.
Persistent Lineage: Even if units are visually "stacked" in a UI, they internally retain their unique lineage
.
Historically Grounded: After VS13, the world no longer contains "items," but "historically grounded material entities"
.
3. Canonical Source Types
To maintain the integrity of the world's matter, all materials must be classified into one of four origin types:
A. Harvested Materials: Derived from world extraction (mining, environmental resources)
. These must include a harvest_event_id, world_location_id, and cycle_id
.
B. Dropped Materials: Resulting from system/entity resolution (NPC drops, event rewards)
. These require a drop_event_id and a resolution_context
.
C. Crafted Materials: Originating from the transformation of existing materials (VS12)
. No spontaneous creation is allowed; they must reference a specific transformation rule and a list of consumed lineage inputs
.
D. Issued Materials: Rare institutional actions for corrections or migrations
. These are performed by Agents (VS7) and must be strictly constrained and audited to prevent invisible economic distortions
.
4. Event Store & Schema Strategy
In VS13, the Event Store is the ultimate authority for the existence of matter
.
Required Material Events
The system must emit the following events to explain every reality transition:
MaterialHarvested / MaterialDropped
MaterialSplit / MaterialMerged
MaterialConsumed / MaterialTransformed
MaterialIssued
Data Schema (Reference Chains)
To ensure performance while maintaining full history, the "True Store" uses reference chains rather than storing full trees inline
:
materials: Tracks stable identity, type, origin_event_id, source_type, and cycle_id
.
material_lineage: A join table mapping child_material_id to parent_material_id to define the transformation or split/merge relation
.
5. System Synergies
VS7 Governance: Authority actions (issuing materials) become a visible, inspectable anomaly class
.
VS10 Scoreboards: Allows for accurate computation of material extraction metrics, rarity statistics, and economic flow analysis
.
VS12 Crafting: Provides the mandatory "input legitimacy" required for reality transformation
.
6. VS13 "Done" Definition
A successful implementation of VS13 is verified when:
Every material unit has a mandatory origin classification
.
Harvests and drops emit material events that the lineage system can query
.
Crafting processes consume real, traceable lineage inputs
.
Spontaneous artifact creation is architecturally impossible
.
Governance-issued materials are fully audited and visible
.
Projections can be fully rebuilt from the event history without losing material truth
