VS9 Specification: Credence & Reputation Layer
VS9 introduces the Social Physics of the IDUNA ecosystem. It is the layer of Institutional Memory, where player behavior is transformed into stable, queryable signals used to interpret risk and trust across the entire digital polity
.
1. Mission and Vision
The Memory of Actors: VS9 is not about "karma" or "gamified dopamine loops." It is a bureaucratic record of an actor's reliability and conduct over time
.
Predictive Governance: It provides the "stability signals" necessary for Agents (VS7) and system processes to make informed, predictive decisions rather than purely reactive ones
.
Continuity of Character: While worlds may reboot via Auraborialis (VS8), an actor's institutional biography persists across cycles
.
2. The Credence Stack (Core Metrics)
VS9 focuses on four primary axes of institutional memory
:
Reliability Index: Tracks technical and behavioral consistency (e.g., frequency of disconnects, tournament abandons, or griefing patterns).
Governance History: A visible audit trail of a user's interactions with authority, including prior suspensions, warnings, or institutional commendations.
Market Integrity: Analyzes trading conduct within the Market Game (VS3) to detect wash-trading, manipulation, or bad-faith economic behavior.
Agent Credibility: Specifically for Agents (VS7), this tracks their tenure, the quality of their authority actions, and their historical adherence to governance constraints.
3. The "Dossier" Identity Model
In VS9, identity evolves from a simple profile into a Dossier or Archive Record of Existence
.
Institutional Biography: Instead of badges or achievements, the system presents a composed record of an actor’s impact on the world across multiple cycles
.
Cross-Cycle Persistence: This dossier is a first-class world-entity that transcends reboots, ensuring that reputation remains the only thing an actor truly "owns" forever
.
4. Interaction with Governance (VS7)
VS9 is the "eyes" of the Governance & Authority Layer (VS7)
.
Risk Interpretation: Agents use Credence signals to prioritize moderation tasks or to determine the severity of enforcement actions
.
Self-Stabilizing World: By making reputation a visible, institutional factor, the world encourages self-regulation and reduces the need for constant, manual Agent intervention
.
5. Visual Identity: The "Registrar's Desk"
Consistent with the "Back Office" aesthetic, the reputation UI must avoid social media tropes
.
Visual Metaphor: The interface should feel like a Registry, a Catalog System, or a Records Desk
.
Aesthetic Tone: Use Garamond for titles to maintain a ritualistic/archival feel, with Helvetica for the dense, functional metadata of the records
.
The Registry Record: Reputation is displayed in ordered rows and partitioned panels that feel like physical compartments in a filing system
.
6. Event Store Vocabulary
Reputation is entirely derived from the Event Store, ensuring it cannot be "faked" or manually edited without a causal record
.

7. VS9 "Done" Definition
A successful implementation of VS9 is verified when:
The system can generate a Reliability Index based on historical event data
.
A user's Governance History is queryable by authorized Agents
.
The Dossier view is operational, presenting an archival record of an actor's cross-cycle history
.
Reputation metrics are integrated as "stability signals" into Agent moderation workflows
.
The UI adheres to the Institutional/Archival aesthetic, strictly avoiding gamification
.
