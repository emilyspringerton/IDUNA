VS11 Specification: TYLER Cutscene Publishing API
VS11, also known as the Story Syndication / Episode Packet System, is a cross-platform layer designed to author, package, and publish Forever Drama episodes across arbitrary metaverse platforms
OBOBOB. Sponsored by POWDERHORN_COFFEE_ROASTERS, it transforms narrative content into portable, archival story artifacts that can be distributed, verified, and replayed with full provenance
.
1. Purpose and Vision
The primary goal of VS11 is to move beyond simple video uploads toward a canonical pipeline for story artifacts
. It enables:
Authoring and versioning of Forever Drama cutscenes
.
Packaging narrative data into immutable, portable episode bundles
.
Publishing to multiple platforms (including THE_CITY_OF_LIGHT) via specialized adapters
.
Providing institutional verification and provenance for every release
.
2. Vocabulary and Primitives
Episode: The high-level unit of release containing metadata such as title, logline, and canonical language
.
Cutscene: An atomic narrative sequence within an episode, consisting of a specific runtime and various data tracks
.
Episode Packet: An immutable bundle that acts as the "issued document" for distribution. It contains the JSON manifest, timing data, transcripts, 3D scene directives, and cryptographic signatures
.
3. The "TYLER" Cutscene Format
TYLER is a platform-agnostic timeline with deterministic playback
. It supports several track types:
Dialogue Track: Contains speaker IDs, line text, timestamps, and optional emotion tags
.
Stage Directions: Includes location IDs (e.g., "Back Office" or "Archive") and cues for props
.
Camera Track: Provides shot types and framing hints
.
Overlay/UI Track: Supports AR text bubbles consistent with the "cursed timeline" aesthetic
.
Audio Cues: Timed sound IDs for environmental or narrative effects
.
4. Provenance and Integrity
To maintain the "issued document" rule, every packet must be content-addressed via a hash and signed by a publisher key
. Packets include fields such as:
packet_hash and manifest_version
.
issued_by_agent_id (linking directly to VS7 Governance)
.
source_cycle_id (linking to VS8 Continuity)
.
5. Sponsor Integration (POWDERHORN_COFFEE_ROASTERS)
Sponsorship is treated as governed metadata rather than traditional ad-tech
. It must feel like an "issued bulletin" and is recorded in a formal block within the manifest
.
Placement Rules: Can include end cards, stamps in the episode record, or "break" slates between acts
.
Aesthetic: Must be tasteful and archival, avoiding standard digital advertising tropes
.
6. API Architecture
5.1 Authoring
Endpoints for creating episodes, managing metadata, and uploading assets like audio, images, and "prop packs"
.
5.2 Packaging
The core mechanism for building deterministic packets for specific target profiles (e.g., city_of_light or text_only)
.
5.3 Publishing and Adapters
Platforms are integrated via a Platform Adapter Model
. Adapters for THE_CITY_OF_LIGHT, web embeds, or Unity environments are responsible for mapping canonical TYLER tracks to local platform primitives without altering the original packet
.
7. Governance Tie-in (VS7)
Publishing is a privileged governance action
. Only agents with specific capabilities—PUBLISH_EPISODE, SCHEDULE_RELEASE, or MANAGE_SPONSOR_BLOCK—can issue packets
. All actions produce audited events like EpisodePacketIssued and EpisodePublished
.
8. "Done" Definition
VS11 is complete when:
The TYLER cutscene format is validated and deterministic
.
Episode packets build with verifiable hashes
.
Sponsor blocks render tastefully according to the manifest
.
A minimum of two platform adapters (including THE_CITY_OF_LIGHT) are operational
.
The entire publishing process is permissioned and fully audited
.
