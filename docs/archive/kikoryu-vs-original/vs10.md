VS10 Specification: Scoreboard / Records Projection Layer
VS10 represents the "Recognized Narrative of Reality" within the IDUNA ecosystem
. It is the layer where raw event data is transformed into socially authoritative projections, providing a visible and stable projection of institutional memory across all game systems and governance actions
.
1. Core Function and Vision
Scoreboards in IDUNA are not merely UI decorations or "gamified dopamine surfaces"; they are canonical reality summaries derived exclusively from the event archive
. They serve as the shared reality anchors that provide institutional legitimacy to outcomes in the world
.
Projection, Not Truth: Scoreboards are derived projections; they are never the primary source of truth and are always recomputable from the Event Store
.
Archival Aesthetic: The visual language must avoid "flashy esports overlays" in favor of a registry feel, ordered rows, and mechanical rhythm consistent with "reading institutional records"
.
2. Canonical Design Principles
Scoreboards must adhere to the "Back Office" aesthetic of an immortal institution
.
Visual Metaphor: Think of scoreboards as ledgers, filing systems, and tabulated records
.
Styling Rules: Use muted tones and structural geometry. Avoid giant glowing numbers, animated gradients, or loud color coding
.
Institutional Authority: Because they carry psychological authority, they must be generated only from server truth and protected from any client-side manipulation
.
3. Structural Rules
Immutable and Derived: Scoreboards are never manually edited. Any correction must occur via a new event emission and a subsequent projection rebuild
.
Cycle-Aware (Auraborialis Integration): All scoreboards are bound to a specific cycle_id from the VS8 reboot protocol
. This ensures that when the world resets, the old cycle's scoreboards are preserved as archived records
.
Snapshot Logic: For stability and replay safety, the system generates snapshots—stable summaries of state at a specific point in time
.
4. Scope Across Systems
VS10 unifies the entire civilization stack into a single conceptual layer
.
VS2 Poker: Records final placements, historical wins, and stack rankings
.
VS3 Market Game: Tracks season rankings, percentage returns, and drawdown metrics
.
MMO / World State: Summarizes event results, faction standings, and territory states
.
VS7 Governance: Provides institutional activity summaries and agent enforcement statistics
.
VS12/VS13 Materials: Records rare constructions, economic impact metrics, and material extraction statistics
.
5. Technical Architecture
The data model uses projection tables to accelerate queries and ensure stability
.
Core Schema Components
scoreboard_snapshots: Stores the computed metadata, metrics, and a payload_json of the result at a specific time
.
leaderboard_entries: Tracks individual ranks and primary/secondary metrics linked to a specific context (season, tournament, or world)
.
contexts: Defines the target for the scoreboard, including the game type, cycle_id, and status (e.g., in_progress, complete, archived)
.
6. API Strategy (OpenAPI)
The IDUNA Scoreboard Service provides a read-only API for clients and a privileged API for administrative governance
.
Public Discovery: Clients can list available contexts and query the latest published scoreboard for any given context
.
Archival Access: Users can query historical snapshots to view prior cycles or completed tournaments
.
Identity Resolution: Identity references in scoreboards can include a user's handle and avatar, subject to privacy and governance rules
.
7. Governance and Administration
Scoreboard management is a privileged action restricted to Agents with specific capabilities
.
Recomputation: Admin endpoints allow Agents to enqueue recompute jobs for projections, typically used after a logic update or a state correction
.
Official Publication: Once computed, an Agent must explicitly publish a snapshot to make it the official recognized narrative for that context
.
Auditability: All recomputations and publication actions are recorded in the governance log for full accountability
.
